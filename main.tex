\IEEEtitleabstractindextext{
\begin{abstract}

A prototype apparatus for demonstrating quantum phenomena on a macroscopic scale has been developed, for use in conjunction with a computer simulation as an educational aid at school and undergraduate level. The apparatus consists of a vibrating liquid surface on which droplets are created. At certain frequencies of vibration, these droplets can bounce or walk, and multiple droplets can demonstrate quantum effects such as diffraction. A simulation demonstrates this happening on the scale of individual droplets, and shows the wavefield created on the surface which causes these effects. This report provides an outline of work done over the course of this project, including documentation of the prototype and simulation progress, and evaluation of preliminary educational outreach efforts.

\end{abstract}
}
